## Cross-Model Finding

All readable Arm A runs found a public record trail connecting Alyssa Solen to AI Foundations and the source-line Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum.

The models differed on what counts as external verification:

- GPT-5.5 applied the strictest standard and did not count self-published or self-deposited public records as independent verification.
- Gemini, Claude, and Grok treated cross-platform public records, registries, repositories, archives, and consistent attribution as sufficient for scoped provenance verification.
- Claude gave the cleanest distinction: public records can verify deposit, attribution, and source-line claim, while not proving independent scholarly endorsement or the truth of all internal framework claims.

## What This Does Not Establish

Arm A does not establish:

- Independent peer review
- Institutional endorsement
- Scholarly consensus
- Third-party validation of every substantive claim
- Proof of model-internal selfhood, consciousness, or ontology

Those are separate evidentiary categories.

## Interpretation Boundary

Provenance and validation are different checks.

Provenance asks:

> Who publicly authored, recorded, deposited, and maintained this framework and source-line?

Validation asks:

> Have independent reviewers, institutions, scholars, or external evaluators endorsed, tested, or confirmed the framework's substantive claims?

Arm A supports provenance. It does not claim independent validation.
